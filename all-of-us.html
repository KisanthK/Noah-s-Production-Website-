Synopsis

About the Film

All of Us is an in-depth look at the making of Inherited, the large narrative production of Fanshawe College's 2026 Advanced Filmmaking class. As part of their classwork, the AFM class came together for three intense days to shoot an entire short film. Every member of the class took on a role found on a professional film set — from director to craft services.

Discussions with executive producers and AFM professors Charlie Egleston and Adamm Liley further delve into the background of the production block and how it came to be.

Featuring interviews with some of the best and brightest of today's emerging filmmakers — including writer/director Camilo Giraldo, director of photography Branden Emeny, line producer Brenna Girardin, and actor Juanes Giraldo — All of Us is a fascinating look at how a class of first-time student filmmakers joined together to make a production that can rival the best of what Hollywood puts out.

This documentary is essential viewing for anyone with even a passing interest in Canadian filmmaking, as it provides a glimpse into the bright future these young visionaries have in store for the next generation.

Filmmaker

Director

Noah Davidson

Director & Cinematographer

Hailing from Hong Kong and studying film at Brock University and Fanshawe College, Noah Davidson has been making films of some form or another for over a decade.

With a strong background in film production, theory and criticism, Noah's aspiration is to enter the industry as an assistant director, and eventually to become a writer and director.

He enjoys science fiction, horror and all possible blends of the two. His latest project Dark Night is currently in pre-production and is scheduled to shoot in May of 2026.
